## The Rice Lake Variables That Drive Motor Selection

Rice Lake is a specific lake with specific characteristics. Generic "what outboard should I buy" advice misses the point. Here's what actually matters for this water:

**Wind exposure.** The lake runs 32 km east-west and is wide open. A west wind can build chop quickly across the full fetch. Underpowered setups for the hull get found out on afternoon runs home.

**Shallow water and weeds.** Most of Rice Lake is 6–10 feet deep. Summer weed growth is extensive. FourStroke motors run cleaner through weeds than older 2-strokes, and slower-revving power is easier on weedy props.

**Trolling as the primary technique.** Walleye trolling is the classic Rice Lake fishery, and proper trolling speeds (1–2 mph) are below the idle speed of most main motors. A 9.9 ProKicker handles this correctly. A main motor at idle does not.

**The sunken railway.** There is an abandoned 19th-century railway submerged across the middle of Rice Lake. It sits roughly 4 feet below the surface. It has been wrecking lower units for over a hundred years. Slow down across the middle of the lake until you know the crossing. Chartplotters (Navionics, Garmin, C-MAP) mark it.

**Public ramp access.** Bewdley, Hastings, Roseneath, and [HBW's Gores Landing ramp](/blog/best-marina-rice-lake-ontario) are the main access points. Motor and boat selection should account for trailer size, parking constraints, and ramp slope at the launch you use most.

**Trent-Severn access.** The east end of Rice Lake connects to the [Trent-Severn Waterway](https://parks.canada.ca/lhn-nhs/on/trentsevern). Boats that travel the system need reliable mid-range cruise performance for the lock-to-lock runs.

## Mercury Options by Fishing Use Case

### Walleye Trolling, The Classic Rice Lake Setup

**Recommended:** 16–18 ft aluminum console boat + Mercury 60–90 HP FourStroke main + Mercury 9.9 ProKicker.

The main motor handles runs between spots and the trip home. The ProKicker handles the actual fishing. Without the kicker, you're compromising walleye presentation, main motors don't idle slow enough for proper trolling speed, and the noise doesn't help in shallow water.

### Smallmouth Bass Fishing

**Recommended:** 17–19 ft fishing boat + Mercury 90–115 HP FourStroke or Pro XS + optional kicker or electric trolling motor.

Bass fishing is more run-and-gun. You move between rocky structure, drop-offs, and weed edges. Some anglers run a kicker for slow-speed work; others use a bow-mount electric for positioning. Both work. The main motor choice depends on your hull length and how much top-end speed you actually use.

### Muskie Fishing

**Recommended:** 18–21 ft deep-V or modified-V + Mercury 115–150 HP + often a 9.9 ProKicker.

Muskie fishing means longer days, bigger lures, and sometimes reaching into the Trent-Severn for range. Bigger boats with full gear need adequate HP to plane reliably. The step from 115 to 150 HP earns its cost on heavier hulls.

### Tournament Bass

**Recommended:** 19–21 ft bass boat + Mercury Pro XS V8 (200–250 HP).

Fast acceleration, maximum top speed, tournament-ready. The Pro XS earns its premium here. For anything short of tournament use, the FourStroke at the same HP is the more practical daily choice.

### Family Fishing, Mixed Use

**Recommended:** 16–18 ft aluminum console + Mercury 90–115 HP FourStroke (Command Thrust if the boat is loaded heavy).

More passengers, more gear, slower pace. FourStroke gives you fuel economy and quiet running. Command Thrust gearcase makes a meaningful difference if you're consistently running a full boat.

## Mistakes We See Every Season

**Skipping the kicker.** Two seasons of trolling with a main motor fixes this opinion. Kicker installs are easier as part of a repower than retrofitted later.

**Wrong prop.** A mismatched prop costs you speed, fuel economy, and sometimes motor longevity. We test props on the water during sea-trials at HBW.

**Underbuying HP for the use case.** A 60 HP on a 19 ft muskie rig fully loaded is going to leave you frustrated. Match the motor to the real-world use, not the minimum specification.

**Skipping Command Thrust on heavier hulls.** If you're consistently running a loaded fishing boat, the Command Thrust gearcase makes a real difference in how the boat planes and handles.

**Ignoring electronics integration at the rigging stage.** Adding a chartplotter, fish finder, and VHF radio after the motor install is a second project. Address it at rigging time.

## FAQs

**What HP do I need for a 16 ft aluminum boat on Rice Lake?**
A 16 ft aluminum fishing boat typically works well with 60–90 HP. Check your capacity plate first, it sets the ceiling. Your use case (solo fishing vs. loaded family) sets the right point inside that range.

**Do I need a kicker for Rice Lake?**
For walleye trolling, yes. For run-and-gun bass fishing, optional. For muskie or mixed-use, usually yes. If you fish Rice Lake seriously, a kicker pays for itself in fishing quality.

**FourStroke or Pro XS for Rice Lake fishing?**
FourStroke for most recreational fishing. Pro XS if you run tournaments or want noticeably faster acceleration and top speed. The FourStroke is quieter, slightly more fuel-efficient, and plenty capable for everyday fishing use.

**What size kicker for Rice Lake?**
The 9.9 ProKicker is the standard for Rice Lake fishing boats (16–19 ft aluminum). For larger boats or longer Trent-Severn runs, the 15 HP ProKicker is worth considering.

## FAQs

### What HP do I really need for Rice Lake?

For a typical 16ft aluminum, 40-60HP handles most situations well. If you fish the open areas regularly or load up with gear and friends, consider 75HP or higher.

### Tiller or remote for walleye fishing?

Many serious walleye anglers prefer tiller for the direct control and open transom. It's personal preference, but tiller is very popular on Rice Lake.

### Do I need a kicker motor?

For dedicated walleye trolling, a 9.9HP or 15HP kicker is worth considering. It saves fuel and gives precise speed control. Not essential, but nice to have.

### What prop works best on Rice Lake?

A 4-blade aluminum or stainless prop usually works well. We can recommend specific props based on your boat and motor combination. Bring it by for a test fit.

### Tiller or remote for walleye fishing on Rice Lake?

Tiller is the preferred setup for walleye fishing on Rice Lake. The Mercury Advanced Tiller system gives you throttle, shift, and trim control directly at the handle, essential for constant trolling speed adjustments. One person can fish and run the boat from the stern without needing a console. Tiller is available on Mercury FourStroke models from 2.5HP through 115HP. Remote makes more sense if you're running with family or non-fishing passengers who prefer to sit up front.

### Do I need a kicker motor for Rice Lake?

For dedicated walleye trolling on Rice Lake, a kicker motor is worth having. The standard setup is a Mercury 60-90HP primary motor paired with a Mercury 9.9HP or 15HP ProKicker for slow, controlled trolling. The kicker lets you troll at the precise 1.5-2.5 mph range that walleye prefer without loading up the main engine. The ProKicker Command Thrust handles Rice Lake's weeds well. For bass fishing or general boating, a kicker is optional, most anglers manage fine with just the main motor.

### What's the best motor for walleye trolling on Rice Lake?

The best walleye trolling setup for Rice Lake is a Mercury 75HP or 90HP FourStroke as the primary motor paired with a Mercury 9.9HP ProKicker (Command Thrust) as a dedicated troller. The FourStroke's smooth low-RPM operation provides clean throttle response, while the ProKicker gives precise 1.5-2.5 mph trolling control without straining the main engine. On a smaller 14-16ft boat, a Mercury 40HP Command Thrust FourStroke in tiller configuration is also used successfully for walleye trolling in Rice Lake's shallower weedy areas.

### Do I need a kicker motor on Rice Lake specifically?

A kicker is most useful for walleye trollers who want precise slow-speed control without burning main engine fuel. It's less critical for bass or musky fishing. If walleye are your main target and you troll frequently, a kicker is a genuine upgrade, many dedicated Rice Lake walleye anglers consider it essential once they've run with one. The Mercury 9.9HP ProKicker Command Thrust pairs well with most 60-90HP main motors without adding excessive weight or complexity.

The most versatile boat size for Rice Lake fishing is 16-18 feet. A 16ft aluminum deep-V handles the open south-end water without being too large for shallow bay fishing.
