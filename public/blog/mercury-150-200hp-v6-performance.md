### V6 vs Inline-4: When to Choose Each

**Mercury Inline-4 (75-115hp)**:
- Lighter boats (16-18ft)
- Budget priority
- Light duty use
- Maximum efficiency

**Mercury V6 (150-225hp)**:
- Boats 18-24ft
- Heavier loads
- Watersports use
- Performance priority
- Better resale

**The Crossover Point**: Around 150hp, the V6 platform makes sense for most applications due to better power delivery and longevity under load.

## FAQs

### Is the V6 worth it over the 150hp inline-4?

For boats 19ft+, yes. The V6 provides smoother power, better torque, and handles loads more confidently. The inline-4 150hp is lighter and more economical for smaller boats.

### What is the real-world fuel economy of a 200hp V6?

At efficient cruise (3500-4000 RPM), expect 4-6 MPG depending on boat and load. At WOT, consumption increases significantly. The V6 is among the most efficient in its class.

### Should I get Command Thrust (CT)?

CT is ideal for heavy boats, shallow water, or if you need maximum low-speed thrust. Standard gearcase is fine for most recreational use and is slightly more efficient at speed.

### How does the V6 compare to the Verado?

Both the V6 FourStroke/Pro XS and Verado are naturally aspirated. The V6 is simpler and less expensive. Verado uses larger-displacement V8/V10/V12 powerheads with premium features like Advanced Noise-Free Steering and DTS. V6 is best value; Verado is best refinement.
