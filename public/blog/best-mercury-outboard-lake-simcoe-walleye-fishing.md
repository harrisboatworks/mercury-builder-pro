## Full Article

Lake Simcoe is bigger water than Rice Lake. The lake covers roughly 740 km², wind exposure is meaningful, and afternoon chop on an open-water crossing can get uncomfortable fast. Walleye are often in deeper water, 40 to 80+ feet, which means more gear, longer runs to spots, and a boat that handles open water confidently.

The right Mercury for Simcoe walleye is different from the right Mercury for Rice Lake. More hull, more main motor, same kicker philosophy.

### The Standard Simcoe Walleye Setup

- **Hull:** 17, 19 ft deep-V aluminum or modified-V fishing boat
- **Main motor:** Mercury 90, 150 HP FourStroke (90 or 115 HP for recreational use; 150 HP for heavier loading or bigger water runs)
- **Kicker:** Mercury 9.9 ProKicker for trolling speed control

The 115 EXLPT FourStroke is the sweet spot for most Simcoe walleye anglers. Enough cruise speed to move between the bass in Cook's Bay and the walleye basins, fuel-efficient at trolling speed with the main shut off, and well-matched to typical 17, 19 ft deep-V aluminum hulls.

Most all-in setups in this class run **$20,000, $32,000 CAD at HBW**, before HST.

### Why Simcoe Rewards More Motor Than Rice Lake

Lake Simcoe-specific factors that push the HP recommendation higher than Rice Lake or smaller Kawartha lakes:

**Wind exposure.** Simcoe is open water. Wind builds quickly and waves can reach 3, 5 feet on bad days. Underpowered boats get exposed.

**Depth and structure.** Walleye at 40, 80+ feet require downriggers and heavier trolling rigs. The setup needs to handle the extra equipment load.

**Travel distance.** Getting from Cook's Bay to Kempenfelt Bay to the deeper basins means meaningful open-water runs. Cruise speed matters for both fishing time and safety.

**Tournament fishing.** Lake Simcoe hosts significant walleye and bass tournament traffic. Tournament-grade hulls and performance motors are common.

### Best Mercury for Simcoe Walleye by Use Case

**Recreational walleye trolling (most common setup)** 
17, 19 ft deep-V aluminum (Lund Pro V, Crestliner Tournament, Princecraft) with 90, 115 HP FourStroke + 9.9 ProKicker. The 115 EXLPT is the sweet spot here.

**Tournament walleye fishing** 
18, 21 ft deep-V with 150, 200 HP Pro XS + 9.9 ProKicker. Pro XS earns its premium for tournament work, faster acceleration to spots, better hole shot when loaded.

**Multi-species (walleye + smallmouth + lake trout)** 
18, 20 ft deep-V with 115, 150 HP FourStroke or Pro XS + 9.9 ProKicker. Same setup covers walleye trolling, bass structure fishing, and deeper lake trout work reasonably well.

### Lake Simcoe vs. Rice Lake Setup Differences

For customers who fish both lakes:

| Factor | Rice Lake | Lake Simcoe |
|---|---|---|
| Typical hull length | 16, 18 ft | 17, 19 ft |
| Typical main HP | 60, 115 HP | 90, 150 HP |
| Hull type | Aluminum console | Deep-V aluminum or modified-V |
| Wind exposure | Lower | Higher |
| Walleye depth | 5, 25 ft | 20, 80+ ft |
| Travel distances | Shorter | Longer |
| Kicker importance | High (9.9 ProKicker standard) | High (9.9 ProKicker standard) |

A boat that fishes well on Rice Lake may be underpowered on Simcoe in the afternoon. A boat set up for Simcoe is right-sized for both.

### Common Lake Simcoe Setup Mistakes

**Underpowering.** A 60 HP on a 19 ft deep-V in afternoon Simcoe chop is going to disappoint. Bigger water rewards bigger HP.

**Hull too light.** A 16 ft aluminum is exposed in moderate Simcoe weather. Most serious Simcoe anglers run 17 ft and bigger.

**No kicker.** Walleye trolling without a kicker on Simcoe is a compromise. The 9.9 ProKicker is the standard.

**Wrong electronics for depth fishing.** Simcoe walleye are often deep. Your sonar needs to work reliably at 80+ feet.

**Insufficient positioning gear.** GPS anchor-capable electric trolling motors are valuable on Simcoe for working specific structure, plan for it if you don't have one.

### Do I need a bigger boat for Simcoe than for Rice Lake?

Usually yes. Simcoe is bigger water with more wind exposure. Most serious Simcoe anglers run 17 ft and bigger; Rice Lake fishes well in 16 to 18 ft.

### What HP do I need for Simcoe walleye fishing?

Depends on hull. For 17 to 19 ft deep-V aluminum, 90 to 150 HP. For tournament use, 150 to 200 HP Pro XS. Pair with 9.9 ProKicker.

### Is a kicker necessary for Simcoe walleye trolling?

Highly recommended. Main motors do not idle slow enough for proper walleye trolling. The 9.9 ProKicker is the standard. See our [ProKicker guide](/blog/mercury-prokicker-rice-lake-fishing-guide).

### Should I run FourStroke or Pro XS on Simcoe?

FourStroke is the better value for recreational walleye fishing. Pro XS earns its price for tournament-level performance and faster acceleration to spots.

### Is Lake Simcoe rougher water than Lake Ontario?

Different rough. Lake Ontario is bigger, has more swell, and longer fetch. Simcoe is shallower with shorter, choppier waves in wind. Both are open water that requires confident hulls and adequate HP.

### Can I fish Lake Simcoe with my Rice Lake setup?

Sometimes, with caution. A 16 to 18 ft aluminum console with 90 HP can handle Simcoe in good weather. In wind or chop, you'll wish you had more boat.

### Should I get a tritoon for Lake Simcoe?

For pure cruising and family use, yes some operators. For walleye fishing, deep-V hulls handle Simcoe better than tritoons. Tritoons are great for sheltered Cook's Bay; less ideal for open Simcoe in chop.

### Where do I launch on Lake Simcoe?

Multiple public ramps: Lefroy, Beaverton, Barrie, Keswick, Sutton. Each has different wind exposure and fishing access characteristics. Local knowledge matters.

### What electronics do I need for Simcoe walleye fishing?

GPS chartplotter, sonar capable of 80+ ft depths, side imaging useful for structure. Garmin, Lowrance, Humminbird, and Raymarine all make units that work. Modern Mercury motors integrate with all four.

### When is the best time to fish Simcoe walleye?

Opening day (typically Saturday closest to May 15) is the traditional opener. Fall (September to October) is also strong. Ice fishing season produces bigger fish but is a different sport.
