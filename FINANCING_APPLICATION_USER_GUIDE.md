### Step 3: Employment Information

**What You'll Enter**:
- Employment status (employed, self-employed, retired, etc.)
- Current employer name
- Your job title/occupation
- How many years with current employer
- Employer phone number
- Gross annual income (before taxes)

**If Employed Less Than 2 Years**:
- Previous employer name
- How long you worked there

**If Self-Employed**:
- Business name
- Type of business
- Years in business

**If Retired**:
- Pension/retirement income details

**Tips**:
- Gross income = before taxes and deductions
- Include bonuses and commissions in annual income
- Round years employed to nearest quarter (e.g., 2.5 years)

**Estimated Time**: 3-4 minutes

---

### Step 4: Financial Information

**What You'll Enter**:

**Other Income**:
- Other income sources (pension, investments, rental, etc.)
- Monthly amount from other sources

**Monthly Expenses**:
- Total monthly expenses (estimate)
- This should include all living expenses

**Assets & Liabilities**:
- Total value of assets (savings, vehicles, property)
- Total amount of liabilities (loans, credit cards)

**Existing Loans**:
- For each loan, provide:
  - Type (auto loan, mortgage, credit card, etc.)
  - Monthly payment
  - Remaining balance

**Credit History**:
- Have you ever declared bankruptcy? (Yes/No)
- If yes, provide details (year, status)

**Tips**:
- Be honest and accurate - lenders verify this information
- Include all debts, even if small
- Don't forget car loans, student loans, and credit card balances
- Assets include cash, investments, vehicles (current value), real estate

**Estimated Time**: 5-7 minutes

A: There's no minimum score requirement, but your credit history is a factor in the decision.

A: Yes, but minimally. Applying for credit typically results in a small, temporary decrease (5-10 points).

A: Yes. A co-applicant is optional and may improve approval chances but is not required.

**Q: What if I'm self-employed?**
A: We accept self-employed applicants. You'll need to provide business information and income details.

**Q: What if I have bad credit or bankruptcy?**
A: You can still apply. Each application is reviewed individually. Be honest about your credit history.

**Q: How much down payment do I need?**
A: Down payments are not always required, but a larger down payment may improve your approval chances and terms.
